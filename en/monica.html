MÓNICA BATE
Anatomy for the movement: line 2
PAPER CIRCUIT WITH ATTINY85 MICROCONTROLLER AND NITINOL OF 0,006". 3 TO 6 ILLUSTRATIONS PRINTED ON PAPER INSPIRED ON NITINOL MOVEMENTS.
Anatomía para el movimiento: línea 2, is a project that stems from the observation of a material reactive to the electric pulse: a nickel and titanium alloy that when exposed to electricity twists and shrinks. The study of the rhythm temps the observer to register the movement as the muscle anatomy of living bodies are represented. Certain visual codes of scientific illustration are even imitated. In this sense, the sequential illustration of what is undergoing movement becomes a language related to the living body as well as a representation that translates time into space.
Mónica Bate (Chile, 1978). Artist based in Santiago. Her work has been exhibited in Chile and abroad. Some of her major projects are: Tape Drawings, IS3, the collective Strings and The Life of Crystals, among other. She specialized in New Media at the UCH, Harvestworks and the ITP (NYU). She is a member of the Anilla Cultural at the Museo de Arte Contemporáneo in Santiago and is co-founder of Laboratorio Flores, an art and science interdisciplinary initiative.
