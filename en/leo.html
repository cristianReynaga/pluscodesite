LEONARDO SOLAAS
Deferment exercises
INTERACTIVE INSTALLATION
Two ways of building an image in real time that face and look at each other. On one side, what stands still; on the other, what moves leaving just a trace. In between, an area of tension between movement and quietness, between action and contemplation. It is a space of deferred meetings, where the observer leaves his image next to the others that were already there, in two collaborative paintings which emerge as a sediment of moments gone. They are brief deferment exercises on the unavoidable disappearance of things passing by.
Leonardo Solaas (Argentina, 1971) studied philosophy at the Universidad de Buenos Aires. He is a self-taught programmer, designer and artist. He has participated in many national and international exhibitions, and has been awarded several prizes and distinctions . He is particularly interested in the production of form and image using generative systems, algorithms and rule sets.
